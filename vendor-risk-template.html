TPRM Program Overview
Third-party risk management under OCC Bulletin 2013-29 requires a risk-based lifecycle approach to vendor oversight — from initial due diligence through contract execution, ongoing monitoring, and termination. This framework reflects the program architecture built and administered at Arvest Bank.
OCC Guidance Scope: Applies to all third-party relationships that involve critical activities — those that could cause a bank to face significant risk if the vendor fails to perform, including relationships that require regulators' attention. Risk level dictates depth of due diligence and frequency of monitoring.

Risk Classification
Vendor Risk Tiering Framework
Risk tier determines the depth of due diligence, contract requirements, and ongoing monitoring frequency. Classification is based on inherent risk factors including access to critical systems, customer data, and regulatory exposure.
Tier 1
Low Risk
  • No access to customer data or core systems
  • Non-critical operational support
  • Easily replaceable in market
  • No regulatory reporting role
Monitoring: Annual review · DD: Standard checklist · Contract: Basic provisions
Tier 2
Moderate Risk
  • Access to non-sensitive systems or limited PII
  • Supports significant operational function
  • Moderate replacement difficulty
  • Some regulatory compliance role
Monitoring: Semi-annual · DD: Enhanced checklist · Contract: OCC-required provisions
Tier 3 — Critical
High Risk
  • Access to core banking systems or extensive PII
  • Critical to business continuity
  • Difficult/costly to replace
  • Significant regulatory compliance dependency
  • BSA/AML or OFAC-related function
Monitoring: Quarterly + event-triggered · DD: Full package incl. SOC 2 · Contract: All OCC-required provisions + audit rights

Event-Triggered Review Checklist
These events require an out-of-cycle review regardless of scheduled monitoring. Document the triggering event, review findings, and any remediation actions taken.
Vendor acquires or is acquired by another entity
Material regulatory enforcement action against vendor
Security incident or data breach at vendor affecting bank or bank customers
Significant operational disruption — SLA breach, extended outage
Material change in vendor key personnel (CEO, CISO, compliance officer)
Vendor adds new material subcontractor(s)
Adverse media or financial distress indicators emerge
OCC/FDIC examiner inquiry regarding vendor relationship

Remediation Framework
Corrective Action Process
When monitoring identifies vendor deficiencies, a structured corrective action process ensures timely remediation and protects the bank from regulatory exposure. Documentation is critical for examination readiness.

Corrective Action Plan — Required Elements
Finding description — specific, factual, and dated
Root cause analysis completed by vendor
Remediation steps with specific owner(s) and target dates
Interim controls documented (if applicable during remediation period)
Validation evidence required for closure (not self-reported)
Risk rating impact assessed — potential tier reclassification
CAP accepted and signed by vendor and bank compliance officer
Logged in vendor management system / examination file
