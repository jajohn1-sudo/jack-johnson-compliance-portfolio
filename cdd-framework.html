CDD/EDD Framework Overview
The FinCEN CDD Final Rule (effective May 2018) codified four core elements of customer due diligence for covered financial institutions — customer identification, beneficial ownership, understanding the nature and purpose of customer relationships, and ongoing monitoring. This framework operationalizes those elements across three risk tiers.
FinCEN's Four Pillars of CDD: (1) Identifying and verifying customers (CIP); (2) Identifying and verifying beneficial owners of legal entity customers; (3) Understanding the nature and purpose of customer relationships; (4) Ongoing monitoring for suspicious activity and updating customer information.
Tier 1
Standard CDD
  • Standard CIP identity verification
  • Basic profile documentation
  • Standard transaction monitoring
  • Annual review cycle
  • No EDD required absent trigger
Risk Score: 0–34
Tier 2
Elevated CDD
  • Enhanced identity documentation
  • Source of funds verification
  • Supervisor approval at onboarding
  • Semi-annual review cycle
  • Heightened monitoring parameters
Risk Score: 35–59
Tier 3
EDD Required
  • Full EDD package before account opening
  • Source of wealth documentation
  • Senior management approval required
  • Quarterly review cycle
  • Enhanced TMS rules and thresholds
  • Relationship documented in EDD file
Risk Score: 60+ OR mandatory EDD category

Mandatory EDD Triggers
High-Risk Customer Categories
These customer categories require EDD regardless of the numeric risk score. Account opening is conditional on completion of EDD requirements and, in most cases, senior management approval. Documentation must be maintained in a dedicated EDD file.
Any customer falling into one of these categories requires EDD prior to account opening. Numeric score is secondary — category designation is the primary driver.
Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs)
EDD Required
Foreign government officials and their immediate family members or close associates — senior political figures, senior military officials, executives of state-owned enterprises. Corruption risk is the primary concern.
EDD RequirementsIdentify PEP status at onboarding and through ongoing screening. Obtain senior management approval. Document source of wealth (not just source of funds). Annual EDD review minimum. Enhanced TMS rules.
Money Services Businesses (MSBs)
EDD Required
FinCEN-registered money transmitters, currency dealers, check cashers, and prepaid access providers. MSBs are themselves regulated BSA entities — verifying their compliance program is a key EDD element.
EDD RequirementsVerify FinCEN registration (MSB Registrant Search). Review states of operation and licensing. Assess agent network size and oversight. Obtain AML/BSA program description. Annual review.
Cannabis-Related Businesses (CRBs)
EDD Required
State-licensed marijuana businesses operating legally under state law but in conflict with federal controlled substances law. FinCEN 2014 guidance requires Marijuana Limited SARs for CRBs regardless of suspicious activity.
EDD RequirementsVerify state license. Confirm no federal enforcement priority indicators. File Marijuana Limited SAR at account opening and quarterly. Assess owner background — no cartel or criminal involvement. Bank counsel sign-off recommended.
Correspondent Banks (Foreign)
EDD Required
Foreign financial institutions accessing U.S. financial system through correspondent relationships. Nested correspondent banking (respondent's respondent) is a key typology risk under PATRIOT Act.
EDD RequirementsUSA PATRIOT Act § 312: full due diligence on foreign correspondents. Anti-shell bank attestation. Nested correspondent analysis. CAMEL/regulatory status review. Prohibition on shell bank correspondents.
Cash-Intensive Businesses
Enhanced CDD
Businesses whose legitimate operations generate large volumes of cash — restaurants, car washes, laundromats, convenience stores, nail salons. Commingling of illicit and legitimate cash is the primary risk.
EDD RequirementsDocument expected cash volumes at account opening. Business license and revenue documentation. Periodic comparison of cash deposits vs. reported revenue. TMS thresholds calibrated to expected pattern.
Nonprofit Organizations (High-Risk)
Enhanced CDD
Charities and nonprofits operating in or sending funds to high-risk jurisdictions — potential terrorist financing conduit risk per FATF guidance. Most domestic nonprofits are standard risk.
EDD RequirementsIRS determination letter (501(c)(3) verification). List of board members — adverse media and PEP screening. Geographic footprint of operations. Purpose and recipient documentation for international transfers.
Private Banking Clients
EDD Required
High-net-worth individuals with complex financial structures — trusts, offshore entities, investment vehicles — where beneficial ownership and source of wealth are more difficult to verify.
EDD RequirementsSenior management approval. Full source of wealth documentation (not just source of funds). Beneficial ownership of all account-related structures. Annual EDD review. Dedicated relationship manager oversight.
Virtual Asset Service Providers (VASPs)
EDD Required
Cryptocurrency exchanges, custodians, and other virtual asset businesses. Rapidly evolving regulatory framework — FinCEN, OFAC, and state-level money transmitter licensing all apply.
EDD RequirementsFinCEN MSB registration verification. State money transmitter licensing review. AML/KYC program assessment. Blockchain analytics capability verification. FATF Travel Rule compliance for transactions ≥ $3,000.

Ongoing Monitoring
Monitoring Thresholds by Risk Tier
Ongoing monitoring parameters are calibrated to customer risk tier and expected activity baseline established at account opening. Standard thresholds below are illustrative — actual institution thresholds are program-specific and set by BSA Officer.
Baseline principle: TMS alert thresholds should reflect the individual customer's documented expected activity profile, not a one-size-fits-all dollar amount. Deviations from the customer's own baseline — not absolute amounts — are the key signal.

Customer Identification Program
CIP Standards — 31 CFR § 1020.220
CIP is the foundation of the CDD framework. Every customer must be verified at account opening using the four required data elements. The verification method — documentary vs. non-documentary — is risk-based.

Beneficial Ownership — 31 CFR § 1010.230
Certification requirement: For all legal entity customers, obtain a completed and signed Beneficial Ownership Certification Form at account opening. Collect for each individual: name, DOB, address, and SSN/passport. Verify consistent with CIP standards.
ProngThresholdWhat to CollectNotes
Ownership ProngEach natural person owning ≥ 25% equityName, DOB, address, ID number, % ownershipZero to four individuals; zero is permissible if no individual owns ≥ 25%
Control ProngOne individual with significant managerial controlName, DOB, address, ID number, title/roleAlways required — even if same as ownership prong individual; typically CEO, CFO, or Managing Member
ExemptionsRegulated entities, publicly traded companies, registered investment companiesConfirm exemption status; document basisExemptions are narrow; confirm entity qualifies before applying
