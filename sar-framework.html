Key money laundering, fraud, and terrorist financing typologies with detection red flags and FinCEN regulatory citations. Content informed by JM FSU Financial Regulation coursework and applied across Amazon FIU and Arvest Bank BSA program.
Structuring / Smurfing
High

Breaking up cash transactions to stay below the $10,000 CTR reporting threshold. Federal crime under 31 U.S.C. § 5324 regardless of source of funds.

  • Multiple transactions just under $10,000 same day or consecutive days
  • Customer expresses awareness of CTR thresholds
  • Transactions at multiple branches or ATMs same day
  • Third parties depositing for same beneficial owner
FinCEN SAR Activity Review — Issue 25 · 31 U.S.C. § 5324
Layering — Wire Transfers
High

Rapid movement of funds through multiple accounts or jurisdictions to obscure origin, typically the second stage of the placement-layering-integration cycle.

  • Funds received and immediately wired out — no commercial purpose
  • Wire activity inconsistent with account purpose / business type
  • Multiple intermediate jurisdictions with no apparent business nexus
  • Round-dollar wire amounts; no invoice or business documentation
  • High-risk jurisdictions: FATF non-cooperative countries
FinCEN Advisory FIN-2014-A005 · FATF Guidance on ML Typologies
Trade-Based Money Laundering
Elevated

Using international trade transactions to transfer value across borders, typically through over/under-invoicing of goods or services.

  • Invoice prices inconsistent with market value (over/under)
  • Multiple alterations to shipping documents
  • Payments from unrelated third parties
  • Transactions involving high-risk trade goods (luxury items, electronics)
FinCEN Advisory FIN-2010-A001 · FATF TBML Typologies
Account Takeover Fraud
High

Unauthorized access to customer accounts to redirect funds, change contact information, or establish fraudulent transfers — common vector at Amazon and retail financial institutions.

  • Contact info changed shortly before large transaction
  • Login from new device / geolocation followed by immediate transfer
  • Customer disputes transaction they did not authorize
  • Multiple failed login attempts preceding successful access
  • Velocity spike in account activity inconsistent with history
FinCEN Advisory FIN-2016-A005 · FFIEC Cybersecurity Assessment Tool
Elder Financial Exploitation
Elevated

Unauthorized or improper use of an elder's funds, property, or assets — reportable under FinCEN SAR guidance for financial institutions. High-priority at community banking level.

  • Sudden changes to account beneficiaries or signatories
  • Third party controlling access / speaking on behalf of elder
  • Large cash withdrawals inconsistent with prior behavior
  • Customer appears confused, fearful, or under undue influence
FinCEN Advisory FIN-2022-A002 · Elder Justice Act
Cryptocurrency / Virtual Asset
Elevated

Use of virtual currency exchanges, peer-to-peer platforms, or mixing services to layer or integrate proceeds. Rapidly evolving typology per FinCEN guidance.

  • Large cash-to-crypto conversions with no business purpose
  • Use of mixing/tumbling services to obscure transaction trails
  • Transactions to/from high-risk exchanges or OFAC-designated addresses
  • Customer unable to explain source of virtual asset holdings
FinCEN Guidance FIN-2019-G001 · FinCEN Virtual Currency Advisory
SAR narrative quality standards aligned with FinCEN guidance. A well-drafted SAR narrative answers the five W's (who, what, when, where, why suspected) with specificity, avoids conclusions, and provides law enforcement with actionable intelligence.
Narrative Component Framework — The Five W's
Component What to Include Common Errors to Avoid
Who Subject's full name, account number, address, occupation, and any related parties (joint holders, beneficiaries, third-party actors). Include entity name and EIN for businesses. Omitting related parties; failing to identify beneficial owners of business accounts
What Specific transaction details: amounts, dates, account types, originating/receiving institutions, and instruments used. Reference CTR filings if applicable. Vague descriptions ("large transactions"); omitting dollar amounts; failing to cross-reference prior SARs
When Transaction dates, detection date, and any relevant timeline (account opening date, EDD review date, prior SAR activity). Date ranges without specifics; failing to note when suspicious activity began vs. when detected
Where Branch location(s), originating country/jurisdiction for wires, ATM locations for cash. For online activity: platform, IP location if available. Omitting location data for multi-branch or multi-jurisdiction activity
Why Suspected Specific red flags observed; departure from established account pattern; customer's explanation (if provided) and why it was or was not satisfactory. Conclusory statements ("activity appears suspicious"); missing customer explanation attempt documentation
Narrative Template — Structuring

Sample SAR Narrative — Structuring

On [DATE(S)], [SUBJECT NAME] ([ACCOUNT TYPE], account #[XXXX], opened [DATE]) conducted [NUMBER] cash transactions totaling $[AMOUNT] over [TIME PERIOD]. Individual transactions ranged from $[LOW] to $[HIGH], consistently falling below the $10,000 CTR reporting threshold. Transaction detail: [LIST DATES AND AMOUNTS — e.g., "01/05/2026: $9,800 cash deposit; 01/06/2026: $9,500 cash deposit; 01/07/2026: $9,200 cash deposit"]. The subject's account profile indicates [OCCUPATION / BUSINESS TYPE]. This level of cash activity is inconsistent with the customer's stated business purpose and prior account history, which averaged [PRIOR PATTERN — e.g., "$2,000–$4,000 per month"]. When contacted regarding the transactions, the subject [stated / was unable to provide / did not respond with] a satisfactory explanation for the activity. No CTR exemption on file. [Prior SAR filed: Yes/No — reference SAR # if applicable.] This activity appears consistent with structuring as defined under 31 U.S.C. § 5324.
Quality Checklist Before Filing
All five W's addressed in narrative
Transaction dates and amounts are specific
Customer explanation attempt documented
Prior SAR activity cross-referenced
No conclusory language (e.g., "money laundering")
Suspicious Activity Type field matches narrative
Supporting documents attached / referenced
BSA Officer review and signature obtained
Filing within 30-day window confirmed
SAR tipping-off prohibition acknowledged
