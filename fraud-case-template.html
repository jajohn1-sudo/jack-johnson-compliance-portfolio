Evidence Collection & Documentation
Evidence Log
Log all evidence collected during investigation. Each piece of evidence requires a source, retrieval date, and analyst signature. Evidence log is the chain-of-custody record — critical for SAR narrative support and any law enforcement referral.
Chain of custody note: Evidence should be retrieved and logged contemporaneously. Do not rely on memory. Document the exact source, date/time retrieved, and any limitations (e.g., "data limited to 90-day retention window").

Referral Decision Framework
SAR & Law Enforcement Referral Decision Tree
Walk through the decision tree to determine SAR filing obligation and law enforcement referral. Each decision is logged for case file documentation. Reset at any time to revise.
Reminder: SAR tipping-off prohibition applies (31 U.S.C. § 5318(g)(2)). Do not disclose to the subject or any person that a SAR has been or may be filed. Refer all subject inquiries to Legal and BSA Officer.
Q1. Does the transaction(s) or activity involve a known or suspected violation of federal law?
Includes money laundering, structuring, fraud, terrorist financing, OFAC violations, and other predicate offenses under the BSA and Bank Fraud Act.
Q2. Does the transaction involve $5,000 or more (or $2,000 or more for money laundering)?
FinCEN SAR filing thresholds: $5,000 for most fraud; $2,000 when money laundering is suspected or $25,000 for computer intrusion.
Q2-alt. Does the activity, while not a known law violation, involve unusual or suspicious activity inconsistent with the customer's profile?
SAR may still be required for activity that raises suspicion even without confirmed law violation — "no known explanation" standard.
Q3. Has the investigation been completed and does the evidence support the suspicion?
Evidence must be sufficient to support a SAR narrative. File within 30 days of detection; 60 days if identity of subject is unknown.
Below filing threshold — enhanced monitoring recommended
Document the activity in case file. Place on 90-day enhanced monitoring. Aggregate with any future activity — filing threshold may be reached cumulatively.
Enhanced Monitoring — No SAR at this time
Q3-alt. Does the activity meet SAR filing thresholds and does the anomalous pattern lack any credible explanation?
SAR Filing Required
Complete SAR package and submit to BSA Officer for review. File via FinCEN BSA E-Filing System within 30 days of detection (60 days if subject unknown). Do not disclose to subject.
SAR Required — File within 30 days
Q4. Does the activity involve imminent risk or is there evidence of ongoing fraud requiring immediate law enforcement notification?
SAR Required Law Enforcement Referral
Coordinate with BSA Officer and Legal on law enforcement notification. Document referral in case file. Continue SAR filing — law enforcement referral does not substitute for SAR.
SAR Required — No Immediate LE Referral
File SAR within required window. Continue monitoring. Reassess law enforcement referral if additional activity is detected post-filing.
Continue Monitoring — No SAR at This Time
Document findings, rationale for no SAR, and monitoring parameters. Set 90-day review trigger. If new activity emerges, reopen decision tree from Q1.
No SAR — Activity Explained and Consistent
Document customer explanation, supporting evidence, and basis for closure. BSA Officer sign-off required for case closure. Maintain case file per retention policy.
Case Closure & Final Documentation
Case Disposition
Select the case disposition outcome and complete the closure checklist. All cases require BSA Officer or supervisor sign-off. Case files must be retained per BSA record retention requirements (5 years minimum).
Disposition Outcome
SAR Filed — Closed
SAR submitted to FinCEN. No ongoing monitoring required unless new activity emerges. Case file retained.
SAR Filed — Continued Monitoring
SAR filed; case remains open for ongoing monitoring. Review trigger set per risk tier schedule.
SAR Filed — Law Enforcement Referral
SAR filed; formal LE referral coordinated through BSA Officer and Legal. Cooperative relationship maintained.
No SAR — Enhanced Monitoring
Activity did not meet SAR threshold. 90-day enhanced monitoring initiated. Case re-evaluates on trigger.
No SAR — Activity Explained
Customer explanation reviewed and accepted as credible. Activity consistent with profile. Case closed.
Account Action — Closure / Restriction
Account closed or access restricted based on investigation findings. Document basis thoroughly.
